VR Medical Training Privacy Simulator

Adjust the privacy settings below to see how they affect data security in a medical VR training environment. Can you find the right balance between functionality and privacy protection?

Data Collection Settings

Biometric Data (Eye movements, heart rate) ⓘ Tracks precise eye movements, pupil dilation, heart rate, and other physiological responses during training sessions.
Performance Metrics ⓘ Collects data on procedure times, accuracy, errors, and decision-making patterns during medical simulations.
Training Room Video Recording ⓘ Records video of the trainee in the physical space while using VR, which may include other people or sensitive information in the background.

Encryption Settings

End-to-End Encryption ⓘ Data is encrypted on your device and can only be decrypted by authorized recipients. No one in the middle, including the VR platform provider, can access the unencrypted data.
Local Storage Only (No Cloud) ⓘ Data remains only on the local institution's servers and is never transmitted to cloud services, significantly reducing exposure to external breaches.
Data Anonymization ⓘ Personal identifiers are removed from the data, replacing them with random codes that cannot be traced back to individuals without a separate, secured key.

Data Sharing Settings

Share with Medical Researchers ⓘ Allows anonymized data to be shared with approved medical research institutions to improve training methods and medical education.
Share with VR Developers ⓘ Provides usage data to the VR system developers to improve software functionality, user experience, and training algorithms.
Share with Commercial Partners ⓘ Allows data to be shared with pharmaceutical companies, medical device manufacturers, and other commercial entities that may use it for product development or marketing.
